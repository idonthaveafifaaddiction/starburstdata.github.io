    Migrating to Starburst Enterprise 352-e or higher#

    The Starburst Enterprise platform (SEP) 352-e release is the first release using the renamed upstream project - Trino (formerly PrestoSQL). As a result a number of binaries, paths, properties, and other aspects now use the new name.

    Users of SEP versions 350-e and lower, need to ensure to review this migration guide, and take the suggested measures in the upgrade process.

    You can find more information in the migration blog post on the Trino website. SEP-specific help and details about the relevant changes in SEP is available in the following sections.

    General advice#

    Upgrading software always is an important step to gain access to new features, bug fixes, performance improvements and other enhancements of the software.

    To understand the scope of the changes, it is important to review the release notes of all the versions. This means you need to have a look at multiple release notes, especially if you are jumping from an older LTS release.

    When choosing your upgrade path and strategy, you need to take into account how business critical your deployment of SEP is to your company. Performing a test upgrade in a non-critical environment is generally advisable, and especially advised when making the complex update jumping from SEP <= 350-e to SEP >= 352-e.

    In the next sections you can learn about the changes affecting different users of SEP, and the required steps to adjust.
